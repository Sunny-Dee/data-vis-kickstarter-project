Kickstarter Campaigns

Analysis of 4,000 most backed Kickstarter projects in 2016.

About Kickstarter

  • Each project is meant to have a specific goal like publishing a book, board game, printing art. Something that needs to be completed.
  • The creator(s) of the project set a funding goal that should include the cost to achieve their goal (labor, materials, shipping expenses, Kickstarter fees, etc.).
  • Projects are all or nothing. If a project doesn’t reach its goal, creators don’t get anything. That’s why backers make a pledge. The money is only charged if the project reaches its goal at the end of the period.
  • Creators can set the duration of the pledge time but the site says that projects longer than 60 days are rarely successful. They recommend a 30-day fund raise.
  • Projects live on the site forever. Even after the pledge period ends
  • Project creation is currently available to individuals with bank accounts in the US, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, the Netherlands, Denmark, Ireland, Norway, Sweden, Germany, France, Spain, Italy, Austria, Belgium, Switzerland, Luxembourg, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Mexico

Social media has democratized access to funding. Sites like Kickstarter, Indiegogo, and GoFundMe have made it easier for people who have an idea to find backers willing to support it with their dollars. We were interested to see if we could find trends related to crowdfunding. Are successful campaigns those that are generated in a certain place? Are there other countries outside the U.S. also using crowdfunding as a platform? What type of project gets the most backers? Is the funding goal highly related to the amount of money and backers a project gets?

For this project, we are using 2 datasets. The first one analyzes 4,000 Kickstarter projects at the end of 2016 and the second one analyzes the 4,000 most funded projects since the beginning of Kickstarter in 2009.

Analysis

The above graph shows projects by location. The size of the glyph represents the number of projects generated in that area. The area grouping is by city. The bottom panel allows the map to filter by country.



Most backed projects by category

The following vis shows a treemap of the 4,000 most successful Kickstarter campaigns by category. The bottom left panel shows the median amount pledge per project for each category. The bottom right panel shows the total amount pledge divided by the initial goal to calculate a multiplier. This measure conveys excitement from backers.

The dashboard allows for specific category comparison. Use the filter to select a few categories of interest and how they compare to each other. For example, product design is the most pledged category, however other categories such as typography exceeded its pledge goal many more times. Typography’s multiple is 29 compared to product design of 9.

Choose 2-4 categories from the checklist to the right to compare.



Categories by country

We divided the countries in similarly sized tiers to avoid clutter. The tiers were determined based on the total sum of dollars pledged for that country. The width of the bar represents the amount pledged in relation to the other countries in that tier. The tooltip tells the user details about the amount of dollars pledged in total and for each category.

We can see that as we go down to lower tiers there is more specialization. Countries use Kickstarter for fewer reason and mostly related to technology.
